## Verdict

The section is strong on decomposition and numbers, but the causal story still outruns what the model evidence in the text actually identifies.

## Findings

1. `56_sectoral_channels.tex:3` overstates the generality of the result. The opening language reads like a universal law of tariff transmission, while the section mostly shows a benchmark-specific calibrated decomposition.
2. `56_sectoral_channels.tex:9,43-47` leans on exact first-order additivity without foregrounding that the decomposition is local to the linearization point and tariff size.
3. `56_sectoral_channels.tex:49-53` shows where the US spillover mass sits, but not yet the mechanism that sends it there. The text should distinguish whether the propagation margin is input-output linkage, expenditure switching, labor-income compression, or some combination.
4. `56_sectoral_channels.tex:77-79` merges observed bilateral trade margins with the interpretation about dollar invoicing. The paragraph should separate trade diversion, delayed Chinese re-direction, and the DCP/ROW channel more explicitly.
5. `56_sectoral_channels.tex:75-88` is overloaded: it moves from sectoral incidence to bilateral trade margins to invoicing-regime interpretation too quickly.

## Hard Seminar Questions

1. What exact model margin generates the negative US spillovers to large service sectors?
2. Would the sign-reversal story survive if sectoral contributions were reweighted by raw value added instead of GDP-contribution weights?
3. How much of the euro-area result is true trade diversion versus a pricing/invoicing artifact?
4. If the ROW margin were shut down, would the invoicing result in the euro area still hold?
5. Is the main claim about sectoral incidence robust, or is it mainly a feature of the benchmark calibration?

## Strengths

- The section is numerically disciplined and consistently ties claims to magnitudes.
- The US/China/euro-area comparison gives the argument a clean structure.
- The spillover matrix is a useful visualization because it exposes gross reallocations that the aggregate bars hide.
