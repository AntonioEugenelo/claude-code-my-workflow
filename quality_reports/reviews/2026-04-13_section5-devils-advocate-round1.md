## Challenges

1. The opening still presents a benchmark-local result as if it were a general tariff-transmission law. Suggested fix: qualify the result as specific to the calibration and linearization. Severity: High.
2. The section compares direct protection with aggregate incidence without defining the metric switch sharply enough. Suggested fix: add one sentence explaining that the comparison is between own-sector gain/loss and net aggregate incidence, and why both are needed. Severity: High.
3. The US spillover discussion remains more compositional than mechanistic. Suggested fix: state explicitly which model margin transmits the contraction through the domestic network before the numerical examples. Severity: High.
4. The service-sector concentration result is pooled but not clearly shown to be broad-based across tariffed sectors. Suggested fix: report a compact distributional summary such as the median row share or count of sectors above `50%`. Severity: Medium-high.
5. The China paragraph does not make clear whether the mechanism differs from the US or is the same channel with different incidence weights. Suggested fix: say explicitly whether China is a different propagation mechanism or a broader version of the same one. Severity: Medium-high.
6. The euro-area subsection still blends incidence, trade margins, and invoicing interpretation too tightly. Suggested fix: separate observed margins, regime comparison, and DCP/ROW interpretation. Severity: High.
7. The closing euro-area synthesis lists multiple offsetting channels without ranking them numerically. Suggested fix: identify which margin is largest and which ones are secondary. Severity: Medium.
