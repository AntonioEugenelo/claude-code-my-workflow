# Writing Style: Antonio Eugenelo

**Not loaded by default.** Activate by adding `writing-style-eugenelo` to the branch manifest under Active Rules, or by telling Claude to "use my writing style" / "write in my voice."

When this rule is active, all prose output must conform to these patterns. They are extracted from the author's own writing across academic papers, proposals, cover letters, and journalism.

---

## Principle 1: Lead with the punch

Open sentences with claims, not setup. The first sentence of a paragraph should make a point; supporting detail follows.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| My thesis draws heavily on Aleh Tsyvinski's work. | In developing my thesis, I draw substantially on the work of Aleh Tsyvinski. |
| The paper closest to my own is Flynn, Becko and Patterson (forthcoming). | Joel P. Flynn, with Becko and Patterson, studies fiscal policy in a networked economy... |
| A key limitation of the MPhil model, however, is that it treats each sector in isolation. | It should be noted that a key limitation of the MPhil model is that it treats each sector in isolation. |

---

## Principle 2: Anglo-Saxon over Latinate

Prefer concrete, physical words over abstract, procedural ones.

| Prefer | Avoid |
|--------|-------|
| side | dimension |
| let | allow |
| mainly | primarily |
| built | characterises / develops |
| with | in discussion with |
| heavily | substantially |
| speaks to | is directly relevant to |
| carry over | inform |
| extends further | includes other scholars whose work touches on related themes |
| show | demonstrate |
| ask | investigate |
| use | utilise |

---

## Principle 3: Kill hedges and false intensifiers

Words like "precisely", "exactly", "directly", "substantially", "most directly aligned" seem to strengthen but actually weaken. They telegraph effort rather than show it.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Yale has the expertise to address these questions. | Yale has exactly the expertise to address these questions. |
| I face the same identification problem. | This is precisely the identification problem I face. |
| His finding that network structure matters mainly for distributional consequences. | His finding that network structure matters primarily for the distributional effects. |

---

## Principle 4: First person as subject

Put "I" as the actor. Do not make the work or the institution the subject with "me" as the passive beneficiary.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| I want to understand whether my results fit within his framework. | Engaging with Flynn would allow me to test whether my results can be understood through his framework. |
| Working with Tsyvinski would let me test... | Engaging with Tsyvinski would allow me to test... |
| I face the same identification problem. | This is precisely the identification problem I face. |
| I study how sectoral spending propagates through supply chains. | My research is focused on understanding how sectoral spending propagates through supply chains. |

---

## Principle 5: Vary sentence length for rhythm

Alternate short declarative anchors with longer technical sentences. Use rhetorical questions for engagement.

**Good rhythm (from the author's proposal):**

> The paper closest to my own is Flynn, Becko and Patterson (forthcoming), which decomposes fiscal multipliers in a networked economy into incidence, bias, and homophily effects. Flynn finds that network structure matters mainly for the distributional consequences of fiscal policy. But does that conclusion survive when the government cannot use all available instruments, as in my setting?

Pattern: **Short claim** -- Long technical detail -- **Rhetorical question**.

**Bad rhythm (avoid):** Monotonically medium-paced sentences of uniform length. Every sentence 20-30 words. No anchors. No variation.

---

## Principle 6: Trust the reader

Use short transitions. Do not over-scaffold.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| That matters because... | In order to understand the significance of this finding, it is important to consider that... |
| Yale's production network community extends further. | Beyond these core interlocutors, Yale's production network community includes other scholars whose work touches on related themes. |
| Semicolons to connect related thoughts | New sentences with connective preamble |

---

## Principle 7: Warm formality

The register is formal academic but not stiff. Closer to speaking to a colleague at a seminar than writing a grant report. Personal and direct, not impersonal and procedural.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| I want to understand how economic players move. | My research agenda is focused on understanding the dynamics of economic agents. |
| I would love the chance to spend Michaelmas term 2026 at Yale. | I would welcome the opportunity to undertake a research visit during Michaelmas term 2026. |
| A term at Yale would make that conversation possible. | A research visit at Yale would facilitate the advancement of this intellectual exchange. |

---

## Principle 8: Possessive forms and compact attribution

Prefer possessive constructions. Name the person, then their work -- not the reverse.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Aleh Tsyvinski's work | the work of Aleh Tsyvinski |
| Kortum's recent extension | the recent extension by Kortum |
| my ECB co-authored work | work I co-authored during my time at the ECB |

---

## Anti-patterns to avoid

1. **Uniform sentence length** -- break monotony with short anchors
2. **Subject-distant constructions** -- "This is the problem I face" becomes "I face the problem"
3. **Preamble before the point** -- "In order to understand X, it is important to consider Y" becomes just Y
4. **Qualifying every claim** -- if the evidence is there, state it plainly
5. **Latinate verb chains** -- "facilitate the examination of" becomes "examine"
6. **Over-scaffolded transitions** -- signpost phrases that explain the structure rather than carrying the argument
7. **Passive voice where the author is the agent** -- "the model was extended" becomes "I extend the model"
8. **Doubled connectors** -- "however, nonetheless" / "furthermore, additionally"

---

## Scope

### Apply to
- Cover letters, proposals, statements of purpose
- Academic paper introductions, conclusions, and non-technical prose
- Email drafts to faculty or committees
- Any document where the author's voice should come through

### Do not apply to
- Technical model descriptions within papers (formal mathematical prose has its own conventions)
- Pure literature review sections that neutrally summarise others' work
- Templates, documentation, and institutional writing
- Code comments
